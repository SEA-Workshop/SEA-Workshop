Scaling Environments for Agents (SEA)

NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

Date: Sat Dec 6th   |   Location: San Diego Convention Center, San Diego, USA

The development of intelligent agents – particularly those powered by large language models (LLMs) – has emphasized the critical role of environments in shaping agent behavior and capabilities, especially for achieving end-to-end autonomy. Environments are not merely testing grounds; they are dynamic, interactive contexts that serve as the essential "data" for agents to learn adaptive behavior, complex reasoning, and long-term decision-making skills. Just as scaling model size has led to emergent capabilities in LLMs, we posit that scaling the structure, fidelity, and diversity of environments is essential for unlocking new forms of agent intelligence. Moreover, recent advances in end-to-end reinforcement learning (RL), particularly when paired with LLM-based agents, have made it increasingly viable to train agents through sustained interaction. These agents can now acquire skills, strategies, and planning abilities through environmental feedback, rather than relying solely on imitation learning or static prompt engineering. As we move toward more autonomous, general-purpose agents, the need for scalable, richly interactive, and diverse environments has become both urgent and foundational.

Topics of Interest Scaling Environments for Agents
1
Environment Design and Simulation Task formalization, compositionality, affordance modeling, procedural generation, and simulator integration.
2
Benchmarks and Evaluation Multi-step interaction metrics, generalization testing, open-ended benchmarks, curriculum scaling, and human-in-the-loop assessments.
3
LLMs in Interactive Environments Policy learning, planning, reward shaping, hybrid training (e.g., RLHF, PPO), and fine-tuning through interaction.
4
Tool-Use and Software Environments Agents as programmers, API orchestration, tool scaffolding, software manipulation, and web navigation.
5
Multi-Agent and Social Environments Scaling agent populations, emergent behaviors, communication, coordination, competition, and role dynamics.
6
Embodiment and Grounding Perception-action loops, physical simulation, spatial reasoning, robotics integration, and simulation-to-physical grounding.
7
Sim2Real and Deployment Domain adaptation, real-world API integration, robustness under scale, safety, and large-scale deployment.

1. Long Papers (up to 9 pages)

Long papers are intended for more comprehensive and mature work, featuring:

  • Extensive experimental or theoretical analysis
  • In-depth contributions with conclusive results
  • Complete studies ready for archival publication
Page limits exclude references and any optional supplementary material.

2. Short Papers (up to 4 pages)

Short papers are ideal for:

  • Preliminary or early-stage results
  • Experimental re-analyses
  • Theoretical insights
  • Position papers inviting discussion and feedback

This track is designed to encourage open sharing of emerging ideas that would benefit from community input and interactive discourse.

All accepted papers will be presented in poster sessions. We will also invite up to 4 accepted papers for oral presentations. Select papers will be featured in Spotlight Talks and eligible for the Best Paper Award.

We look forward to your submissions and to fostering an engaging and constructive exchange of ideas!
